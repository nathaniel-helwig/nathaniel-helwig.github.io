Collaborating with My Lab

Options for initiating a productive collaboration:

  • Methods: If you have methods, then I have input. I am open to finding collaborators with methodological interests in modern multivariate and nonparametric methods. Recently, I am particularly interested in machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) methods.
  • Applications: If you have data, then I have methods. I am always looking for collaborators with interesting real data problems that require nonstandard (nonparametric) solutions. I am particularly interested in applications in psychology, medicine, economics, and sports.

See the Publications page for examples of previous methodological and substantive collaborations.

Volunteering in My Lab

Options for becoming a volunteer lab member:

  • U of M Students: If you are an undergraduate or graduate student that is currently at the University of Minnesota, then I strongly encourage you to enroll in PSY 5993 with me to obtain research experience. Interested students should email me to discuss this opportunity.
  • Community Members: If you are a student at another institution (or a non-student) that is interested in getting involved in research, then reach out. I am always looking for new lab members with interests in psychology, statistics, machine learning, and artificial intelligence.

See the Publications page for examples of previous products from student volunteers.
